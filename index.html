Addis Ababa

Addis-capital of Ethiopia, great and vibrant city, that offers great places to visit, great food to eat and kind people to chat with. Once you're in the city check out National Museum, where you can see the history of our humanoid ancestors, after that check out Ethological Museum, based on the territory of the university in old Haile Selassie palace. There is a big amount of churches, so in Addis Holy Trinity Church is a must see. Another must see is Merkato , the biggest market in Africa and Entoto park, where you can admire Addis skyline.Addis is great for socializing, meeting new people, full of culture and life, especially in Piassa and Bole areas. According to Explore Ethiopia it is a must see place, the more time spent the better, as you can then fully start loving this place.
Check link underneath to find more interesting info and places to see in Addis Ababa: Addis Ababa-Lonely Planet


Aksum


Aksum carries the history of the Axumite civilization and connection with the beginning of the Solomonic dynasty through the Queen of Sheba and the place, where allegedly Arc of Covenant is kept. We reccommend visiting the obelisk site, which were created 1700 years ago. You can see the building where Ark of Covenant is kept, but only from outside as no one is allowed inside, apart from the longlife guardian of it. Outside of the city there is a worth to visit the site of the ruins of the palace, legend says thats is the palace of Sheba.
For more information check the link below: Aksum-Lonely Planet


Bahir Dar


Bahir Dar and its lake Tana offers you beautiful scenery and traditional monasteries spread on the little islands on the lake, as well as the amazing waterfalls wherre is the beginning of the Blue Nile. It is beautiful and peaceful.
For more information check the link below: Bahir Dar- Lonely Planet


Gonder


Gondar is a city located north of lake Tana and close to Simiean Mountains. It's castle Fasiil Ghebbi is on the Unesco Heritage list. It has a royal baths, where the ceremonies of Timket are taking place. Oustide of Gondar Simiean Mountains is a must go to, if you like a good hike.Simiean Mountains is a home of gelada monkeys, the only grassfed monkeys in the world and endemic for Ethiopia.
For more information check the link below: Gondar- Lonely Planet

Harar

Harar is one of the most important walled cities for Islam and used to be known as a very important commercial centre. It is very colorful and very different than other cities in Ethiopia. It's great to walk within it's walls, see all the colorful buildings,vast amount of mosques. Amazing to see a typical Harari house in the cultural centre as well.
For more information check the link below: Harar- Lonely Planet

Lalibela

Lalibela is famous for rock-cut monolithic churches. The whole of Lalibela is a large and important site for the antiquity, medieval and post-medieval civilization of Ethiopia. To Christians, Lalibela is one of Ethiopia's holiest cities, second only to Axum, and a center of pilgrimage.
For more information check the link below: Lalibela- Lonely Planet
